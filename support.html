Overview of Image Detection Process

The image detection process uses advanced AI models to identify faces in images. Once a face is detected, the system compares it against the stored "thief" image to identify potential matches. This process is crucial for surveillance systems, identity verification, and many other security applications.

  • Upload a gallery of images and a single image of the suspected thief.
  • The system detects faces in the gallery and compares them to the thief's face.
  • If a match is found, the system highlights the image and stores results for review.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the face detection process?

The face detection process uses cutting-edge deep learning models, but accuracy may vary depending on image quality and angle. We recommend high-resolution, clear images for best results.

What should I do if the system doesn't detect the face?

Ensure the image is well-lit and the face is not obstructed. If the issue persists, try uploading images with different angles of the face for better recognition.

How do I upload the thief's image?

You can upload the thief’s image in the 'Thief Image' section. Make sure the image is of a single face for the best results.

Troubleshooting

  • Check if the images are high quality, well-lit, and have clear facial features.
  • Ensure you upload a single face in the thief’s image to avoid confusion.
  • If faces are not detected, try using different angles or clearer images.
